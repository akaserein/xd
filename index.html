The Problem

Plastic pollution has become one of the most pressing environmental issues of our time. Non-biodegradable plastics accumulate in landfills, choke our water bodies, and release microplastics into the environment. Every year, millions of animals die due to ingestion of plastic waste. Moreover, humans indirectly consume plastic particles through food and water, leading to potential health hazards.

Impact on Environment

üåä Oceans

Marine ecosystems face catastrophic impacts. Sea turtles, fish, and seabirds often mistake plastic for food, leading to injuries and deaths. Coral reefs are suffocated by plastic debris.

üèû Land

Soil fertility reduces drastically when plastics break down into microplastics, blocking nutrient absorption by crops. Farmlands get degraded, impacting food production and farmers' income.

üå¨ Air

Burning plastics release toxic chemicals such as dioxins and furans, contributing to air pollution, respiratory diseases, and climate change.

Solutions & Alternatives

Biodegradable Materials

Promoting materials made from corn starch, jute, banana leaves, and biodegradable polymers as alternatives to single-use plastics.

Recycling & Circular Economy

Encouraging segregation at source, advanced recycling plants, and promoting re-use of plastic materials to minimize waste generation.

Community Awareness

Awareness campaigns, school programs, and public participation can reduce plastic dependency and encourage eco-friendly habits.

Role of STEM

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) provides the foundation to tackle plastic pollution:

  • Science: Researching biodegradable and bio-based materials.
  • Technology: Innovations in waste segregation, AI-driven recycling, and smart bins.
  • Engineering: Designing efficient waste management infrastructure and machinery.
  • Mathematics: Data modeling to track waste generation, optimize logistics, and predict future trends.

Our Initiative

This seminar aims to showcase projects and models under the theme "STEM for Viksit and Atmanirbhar Bharat." By focusing on reducing plastic pollution, we encourage students to present innovative solutions, from biodegradable alternatives to futuristic waste management systems, ensuring a cleaner and greener India.
